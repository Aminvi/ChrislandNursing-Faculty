Meet The Faculty

HEAD OF DEPARTMENT

Professor Idongesit Ibanga Akpabio

Is a Professor of Community Health Nursing and works at the Department of Nursing Science, University of Calabar. She holds PhD and M.Sc. degrees in Nursing obtained from University of Ibadan, Nigeria in 2006 and 2000 respectively. She also holds MPA degree from University of Calabar (1995), B.Sc. degree in Nursing obtained from University of Nigeria, Enugu Campus in 1990 and other Professional Nursing Certificates including Registered Nurse (1997), Registered Midwife (1980) and Registered Public Health Nurse (1990)..

She has held many leadership positions including Acting Head, Department of Nursing Sciences, Deputy Dean, Dean of her Faculty from October 1, 2018 to February 28, 2023. During the period of her Deanship, she had opportunity to serve as the Chairman, Committee of Deans of the University of Calabar and acted several times as Vice Chancellor when the Vice Chancellor and her Deputies were away from Campus. Through these positions, much impact were achieved in the development of many of our Nursing programmes as well as approval for commencement of other academic programmes in the University.

Nursing faculty is divided into Sub-Department

Medical Surgical Nursing

Nursing faculty within the Medical Surgical Nursing department are pivotal in delivering high-quality patient care across a spectrum of medical conditions. Their expertise encompasses pre- and post-operative care, wound management, and precise medication administration. Collaborating seamlessly with physicians and surgeons, they ensure holistic treatment plans tailored to individual patient needs. With specialized knowledge in areas such as orthopedics, neurology, oncology, and cardiology, medical surgical nurses exhibit exceptional critical thinking skills, particularly in emergencies where swift decision-making is crucial.

Maternal and Child health Nursing

In the Maternal and Child Health Nursing department, nursing faculty play a vital role in supporting expectant mothers and ensuring the health and well-being of both mother and baby throughout pregnancy. From offering prenatal education and labor support to providing guidance on postpartum care and pediatric health, these nurses are dedicated to promoting maternal and child health at every stage. Their expertise extends to lactation support, newborn care, and developmental assessments, making them indispensable members of the healthcare team for growing families.

Community Health Nursing

Community health nursing faculty are instrumental in promoting health and wellness within communities through preventive education initiatives and outreach programs. By engaging with local populations and collaborating with community organizations and public health agencies, they address broader social determinants of health such as poverty and access to healthcare resources. Through health promotion campaigns and targeted interventions, community health nurses strive to foster healthier lifestyles and reduce healthcare disparities, making a tangible impact on the overall well-being of the community they serve.
